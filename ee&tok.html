EE & TOK

What is the EE?

The EE, stands for Extended Essay, is a 4,000 word thesis where students prepare and develop new skills for their next big step: university. Students will have to investigate a topic of their interests and they will have an IB tutor (an IB teacher from school) who will be constantly under the supervision of the work. According to the IB, students will develop the following skills.

  • formulating an appropiate research question
  • engaging in a personal exploration of the topic
  • communicating ideas
  • developing an argument

EE structure

The EE must includde the following:

  • A title page
  • Contents page
  • Introduction
  • Body of the essay
  • Conclusion
  • References and bibliography

It is also necessarly that the main topic of the essay falls into one of the 6 groups:

  • Group 1: Studies in Language and Literature
  • Group 2: Language Acquisition
  • Group 3: Individuals and Societies
  • Group 4: Sciences
  • Group 5: Mathematics
  • Group 6: The Arts

How is it graded and how it influences the final graded?

Students will receive a score related to a band, which are:

  • A-work of an excellent standard.
  • B–work of a good standard.
  • C–work of a satisfactory standard.
  • D–work of a mediocre standard.
  • E–work of an elementary standard.

What is TOK?

TOK stands for, Theory of Knowledge and is one of the mandatory subjects in the DP. TOK aims to raise consciousness among students about the nature of information. According to the IB´s official website, it offers students anteachers the opportunity to:

  • reflect critically on diverse ways of knowing and on areas of knowledge
  • consider the role and nature of knowledge in their own culture, in the cultures of others and in the wider world

How is TOK assessed?

Students will have to prepare an oral presentation, which can be individual or in groups of 2 and 3 students per group (approximately 10 minutes per person). Therefore, they will have to write a 1,600 word essay on aprescribed title.

TOK, along with the EE contribute up to 3 additional points toward the total (45 points).

Subjects

First of all, there are a few requirements that must be followed regarding the choice of the subjects:

Choice between subjects HL subjects Group 4 subject
French language and literature, English B and Mathematics are compulsory subjects. Students will have the to choose between Business & Management, Physics and Biology, then between Chemistry and Global Politics, and finally between ESS and ITGS. It is required that the student take minimum 3 subjects in HL. It is compulsory to take at least 1 Group 4 subject in order to do the Group 4 Project.

French Language & Literature (Group 1)

Standard Level & High Level:

French language and literature is a compulsory subject, in which students must choose between SL and HL. In this subject, the main difference between both levels, is that HL students study 6 works, each of them in different forms of literary texts, times and places instead of 4 works. The official assessment compromises two papers: the first one is an analysis of a literary and non-literary text not studied before in class, and the second one is a comparative answer to a question focused on two literary works already studied by the student. In addition, HL students will be assessed by paper 3 which consists of 1200-1500 word essay about one piece studied during class.

Internal Assesment:

The Internal Assesment(IA) consists of an individual oral presentation about 3 works, chosed by the student. The presentation has to last between 10 and 15 minutes and contributes to a 15% of the final grade.

English B (Group 2)

Standard Level & High Level:

English B aims to enable the students to communicate in English in both familiar and unfamiliar environments. The first paper of the assessment consists of a writing from a choice of 3 related to the topics studied by the students all over the 2 years, which are:

  1. Identities
  2. Experiences
  3. Human ingenuity
  4. Social organiation
  5. Sharing the planet

Paper 2 involves two sections of reading and listening. Both levels are assessed equally, however, in HL they will require more complex language and structures, as well as an extended word range.

Internal Assesment:

In this case, there is a difference between both levels. SL students will have to do an oral presentation about a visual image related to the syllabus. Instead, HL student image is from one of the 2 books read in class, which are:

  • The whale rider, Witi Ihimaera
  • 1984, George Orwell

Mathematics (Group 5)

Standard Level & High Level:

In the IB DP is compulsury to take Mathematics classes and will be assessed in French. School offeres 3 types of Mathematics courses:

  • Apllication SL
  • Application HL
  • Analysis HL

The syllabus is the following:

  1. Algebra
  2. Functions and equation
  3. Circular and trigonometry
  4. Vectors
  5. Calculus

In addition the students will have to study one option out of four, which is in this case: Further statistics and probability.

The main difference between application and analysis is the number of hours spend learning about each unit. For example, application students, are most likely to spend more time learning about statistics and probability, and on the other hand, analysis students will learn more about geometry and trigonometry.

Internal Assesment:

Every maths student will have to do an individual exploration related to an area of mathematics, which will normaly take 20 hours.

Biology (Group 4)

Standard Level & High Level:

Biology will be assessed and learned in Spanish. Paper 1 includes 30 multiple questions, paper 2 includes short and extended answers related to the syllabus. In addition, HL and SL students will be assessed by paper 3, which is similar to paper 2. The IB Biology syllabus is the following:

  1. Cell biology
  2. Molecular biology
  3. Genetics
  4. Ecology
  5. Evolution and biodiversity
  6. Human physiology

Moreover, students will have to study one more option out of 4 chose by the center. At school, the option is Human phisiology.

Internal Assesment:

In this case, the IA is an individual investigation. It could be a hands-on experimental work, a computer simulation... It is expected that students spend 20h and it´s a 20% of the final grade for SL and HL.

Business & Management (Group 3)

Standard Level & High Level:

The Business & Management course aims to develop the comprehension of the important theories of business management that nowadays surrounds us. Every class will be learne in Spanish because the final assessment will be in Spanish. In this course, the assessment is divided in 2 papers instead of 3. Paper 1, as well as Paper 2, has a format of structured and extended answers. This subject is divided in 5 units, which are:

  1. Business organization and environment
  2. Human resource management
  3. Finance and accounts
  4. Marketing
  5. Operations management

Internal Assesment:

For the IA, student will have to contact with a real business and they will have to investigate and report on a decision or an issue that the business will have to face.

Physics (Group 4)

Standard Level & High Level:

As well as the previous two subjects, Physics will be assessed in Spanish. The format of the assessment is pretty similar to the Biology assessment. Paper 1 is a 30 multiple-choice questions and Paper 2 and Paper 3 are short and extended answers about the syllabus. The syllabus of the physics classes is:

  1. Measurements and uncertainties
  2. Mechanics
  3. Thermal physics
  4. Waves
  5. Electricity and magnetism
  6. Circular motion and gravitation
  7. Atomic, nuclear and particle physics
  8. Energy production

Internal Assesment:

The IA is for students a write-up report about an independent physical experiment. It is expected that students last 10h on preparing the Physics IA.

Global Politics (Group 3)

Standard Level & High Level:

By choosing this subject students will explore real politics concepts and understand from local to international political activity. During the 2 years program, students will explore 4 units about people, power & politics, more precisly:

  1. Power, sovereignty and international relations
  2. Human rights
  3. Development
  4. Peace and conflict
In addition HL students will have to write a case study approach about 2 of the following 6 global political challenges:
  • Environment
  • Health
  • Identity
  • borders
  • Security
Paper 1 consists of 4 types of different sources and 4 different questions and Paper 2 involves extended answers from the 4 units.

Internal Assesment:

HL students will have to record a 10-minutes presentation where they will talk about their research done in the case study. For SL and HL students it is required a 2000 words, written report on a political issue.

Chemistry B (Group 4)

Standard Level & High Level:

Chemistry is a subject which synthesize academic data and pratical skills. The syllabus of this subject is the following:

  1. Stoichiometric relationships
  2. Atomic structure
  3. Periodicity
  4. Chemical bonding and structure
  5. Energetics/thermochemistry
  6. Chemical kineticts
  7. Equilibrium
  8. Acids and bases
  9. Redox processes
  10. Organic chemistry
  11. Measurement and data processing

The main difference between both levels is the amount of hours and the complexity of the syllabus. Hl students will spend more hours in all the topics and they will go deeper in the studies. Paper one here, is a 40 multiple-choice questions, Paper 2 are short and extended answers and finally, Paper 3 are pratical-based questions, as well as more questions like the ones in Paper 2. In Chemistry there is an additional option, which is Human Biochemistry.

Internal Assesment:

In this IA, students will have to elaborate a chemistry experiment and then write a report about it. As always, it is a 20% of the final grade.

Environmental Systems & Societies-ESS (Group 4)

Standard Level:

ESS is only available in SL, nevertheless is a subject, which can meet either Group 3(Individual and Societies) and Group 4(Sciences). Paper 1 consists of a case study and Paper 2 consists of short answers and structured essays. The syllabus is divided into 8 units which are:

  1. Foundations of environmental systems and societies
  2. Ecosystems and ecology
  3. Biodiversity and conservation
  4. Water and aquatic food production systems and societies
  5. Soil systems and terrestrial food production systems and societies
  6. Atmospheric systems and societies
  7. Climate change and energy production
  8. Human systems and resource use

Internal Assesment:

The student will have to create and research a question, in order to create a written report.

Information Technology in a Global Society-ITGS (Group 3)

Standard Level:

ITGS is offered at HL and SL, however, at Lycée International Barcelone it is only available in SL. The syllabus can be divided in two stands:

Stand 1 Stand 2
Reliability and integrity Business and employment
Security, privacy and anonymity Education and training
Intellectual property and authenticity Environment
The digital divide and access equality Health
Surveillance Home and leisure
Globalization and cultural diversity Education and training
Policies, standards and protocols Politics and government
People and machines
Digital citizenship

Moreover, Paper 1 is assessed by 3 structured responses and in Paper 2 they will have to write an answer to an unseen article.

Internal Assesment:

They will have to create and develop an IT solution for a specific client.
